Welcome to the CS Department

The Computer Science (CS) Department at George Mason University is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of computer science and equipping students with the skills and knowledge needed to excel in a fast-paced technological world. Our department is recognized for its excellent faculty who are deeply involved in groundbreaking research areas such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, data analytics, and software engineering.

Our programs are designed to meet the diverse needs of students, whether they are seeking to launch a career in technology or enhance their skills as professionals in the field. We offer undergraduate, master's, and doctoral programs, all of which focus on providing students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills. Through partnerships with industry leaders and a focus on hands-on learning, students are well-prepared to enter the workforce or continue their education in advanced research. Learn more about our department by visiting our official website.

Available MS Degrees

MS in Computer Science
A program that covers core topics like algorithms, operating systems, and machine learning.
MS in Information Security and Assurance
Focuses on the protection of data and systems through advanced cryptography and network security.
MS in Data Analytics Engineering
Learn to analyze and interpret large datasets using machine learning and data mining techniques.
MS in Software Engineering
Prepares students to design, develop, and manage large-scale software systems.
MS in Information Systems
Combines technical and managerial skills to oversee information systems within organizations.

Required Courses for MS Degrees

  • CS 530: Fundamentals of Computer Science
  • CS 531: Computer Systems and Fundamentals of Systems Programming
  • CS 550: Operating Systems

  • ISA 562: Network Security
  • ISA 656: Cryptography and Data Security
  • ISA 673: Operating Systems Security

  • DAEN 500: Introduction to Data Analytics
  • DAEN 651: Data Mining and Machine Learning
  • DAEN 620: Big Data Processing
